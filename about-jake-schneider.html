Jake Schneider

President, CEO and Founder

Schneider Economics, LLC




Jake W. Schneider is the President, CEO and Founder of Schneider Economics, LLC, a bespoke economic research firm that uses the intersection of tried-and-true analytics with up-and-coming data science techniques. Mr. Schneider is a Master in Public Administration in International Development candidate at the Harvard Kennedy School (2020) where he concentrates on the intersection of economics, public policy and international development. During the summer of 2019, Mr. Schneider served as a Short-Term Temporary (STT) consultant at the World Bank in Washington, D.C. with the Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA). He currently serves as Guest Lecturer at the Thayer School of Engineering at Dartmouth College where he presents a lecture series on big data, data science and improving international healthcare. Prior to his matriculation at the Kennedy School, Mr. Schneider worked at the Brookings Institution where he co-authored four reports on how to increase investment in global health. Previously, Mr. Schneider worked as an analyst reporting directly to Former Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan at Greenspan Associates, LLC and as a research intern in the Office of Economic Policy at the U.S. Treasury Department. Mr. Schneider holds a B.A. magna cum laude with honors from the College of William & Mary in Virginia (2015).
